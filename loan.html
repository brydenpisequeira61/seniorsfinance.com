  • Credit score

    Higher credit scores generally indicate lower risk, while lower scores indicate higher risk. Credit scores can range from 300 to 850 in the United States, with scores above 700 generally considered good or excellent.

  • Know that loans can actually boost credit scores

    taking out a loan and making timely payments can actually boost your credit score. This is because on-time payments are one of the most important factors in determining your credit score, and having a mix of different types of credit (such as installment loans like a mortgage or car loan) can also improve your score. Just be sure to only take out a loan if you can afford the payments and use the funds responsibly. Overborrowing or missing payments can have the opposite effect on your credit score.

  • Understand that there are types of personal loans

    There are different types of personal loans available, depending on the purpose of your loan.

  • Paperwork in order

    All necessary documents and forms should be completed accurately and submitted in a timely manner.

  • Pre approved

    Pre-approved refers to a process in which we have reviewed a borrower's financial information and determined that they are eligible for a mortgage loan up to a certain amount.

  • Know the Terms

    It's essential to understand the loan terms, including the interest rate, repayment schedule, and any fees or penalties.

  • Make a plan to pay it back

    Determine your monthly payment: Based on the loan terms, calculate your monthly payment. This amount will be due each month until the loan is fully repaid.
